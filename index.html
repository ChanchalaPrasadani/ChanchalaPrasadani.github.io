ABOUT

The cultivation of vegetables becomes a prominent income among the farmers in Sri Lanka. The main objective of this research was to find an optimal solution for the transportation problem at an efficient and low cost. This study is aimed to find out challenges in vegetable transportation in the up-country region (Nuwara-Eliya, Badulla, Kandy, and Matale, etc.) and to propose suggestions to mitigate such challenges within the study area to reinforce the socio-economy of the people. Information on farmers, vendors, and transporters was gathered through semi-structured interview phone calls and observations, whereas information that is needed for our product was gathered through a study of relevant literature augmented by key informant interviews. The expected outcome of this project was that the mobile application deals with farmers, vendors, and transport providers. So it should be easy for all three parties to perform their task. Here we tried to solve the main problem faced by traders, wholesalers, and transporters especially in vegetables and fruits through this application, building the connection between each other. This enables wholesale buyers to purchase products faster and at a lower cost. This reduce the need to travel to wholesale centers for buying and selling, without a middle seller man.
